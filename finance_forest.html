Finance Forest

Finance Forest is a solution which addresses the lack of engaging financial education in primary schools, and aims to facilitate financial understanding in an increasingly cashless society. The final product is a tablet application, which is used by educators in the school system as an aid in teaching financial concepts. The application will help children understand financial concepts and subsequently build beneficial monetary habits from a young age, assisting them in the long term. 

User Testing + Prototypes

The design process began with ideation through sketching and collaborative exercises such as affinity diagraming and group passing. After the ideation phase, we created utilised paper prototypes to test with users. 

After several rounds of user testing with low fidelity prototypes, we made a digital version to test wtih our target audience of primary school aged children. This proved very insightful in determining necessary changes, and formed the basis of our decision making process for the high fidelity prototype. 

Lastly, user testing was again conducted with the target user group before the final version was made. The purpose of this course of user testing was to make the final touches. User feedback from this round of testing was very positive, with most children saying they had fun playing Finance Forest, and that they would like to have it implemented in the classroom. 

Functionality

Finance Forest incentivises learning through challenges, which give the player the opportunity to earn in-game money to buy items in the shop, which can be used to customise their tree. Each user has their own individual tree, which contributes to their class forest. The class forest displays the trees and associated profiles of all of the students in a class.

Reflection

It was insightful to see a project through from the ideation phase through to the high fidelity prototypes, We came across several unexpected challenges, such as overestimating children's knowledge of financial terms and their vocabulary more broadly. We also found it challenging to cater the application to children of all ages, starting as young as five years old. 

This project also highlighted the importance of consistency and establishing a cohesive style guide, especially when working with young children who are mostly visual learners. By establishing these guidelines early on in the process, we were able to create a strong brand identity.
